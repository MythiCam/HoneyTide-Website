# HoneyTide Apiary Website Notes

## Project Overview

This is a static HTML/CSS website hosted on GitHub Pages for HoneyTide Apiary.

HoneyTide Apiary is a sole-proprietor beekeeping business owned and operated by Camron Purdum in the Springfield/Eugene, Oregon area.

Camron is the Founder and Head Beekeeper of HoneyTide Apiary.

The website should feel personal, local, and grounded in the real experience of a beekeeper building an apiary from the ground up.

## Brand

Business Name: HoneyTide Apiary

Slogan: Nature’s Rhythm. A Bee’s Blessing.

Location: Springfield/Eugene, Oregon area; Lane County, Oregon.

## Voice and Tone

The website should not sound like a corporation, nonprofit board, or large organization.

The current issue is that some pages say “HoneyTide Apiary” too often and sound too institutional.

The site should feel like visitors are meeting Camron and following the growth of his apiary.

Use more natural language where appropriate:

- I
- my apiary
- my colonies
- my beekeeping journey
- the bees
- the apiary
- HoneyTide

Use “HoneyTide Apiary” when introducing the business, in titles, SEO, footer, and formal references.

Avoid overusing phrases like:

- HoneyTide Apiary is committed to...
- HoneyTide Apiary believes...
- HoneyTide Apiary provides...
- We are committed to...

Prefer:

- I’m building HoneyTide as...
- My goal is...
- As the apiary grows...
- I’m learning...
- I hope to offer...
- The bees are teaching me...

## Current Colonies

Camron currently manages four colonies under HoneyTide Apiary:

- Zion
- Tortooga
- Butera
- The Anchor Colony

Colony notes:

- Zion and Tortooga were HoneyTide’s founding nucleus colonies.
- Zion was very strong from the start and needed to be transferred into a full hive quickly.
- Tortooga has developed more slowly and represents patient, observation-based beekeeping.
- Butera was HoneyTide’s first successful swarm capture.
- The Anchor Colony was recovered from an unusual mailbox-anchor cavity.

## Business Direction

HoneyTide is not only an education/stewardship project.

HoneyTide is being developed into a sustainable apiary that will eventually offer hive products when colonies are healthy and strong enough to share surplus responsibly.

Future offerings may include:

- Small-batch local honey
- Beeswax products
- Other hive-based products where appropriate
- Pollinator education
- Beginner-friendly beekeeping education
- Community outreach
- Swarm awareness and easy-access swarm assistance

Hive products are not currently available for regular sale.

The website should communicate that products are part of the future business plan without making the site feel sales-heavy or misleading.

3. The Home page does not clearly communicate future hive products strongly enough.
4. The Education and Contact pages have some overlapping swarm/contact information.

6. The Products side of the business is underrepresented.

## Page Direction

### Home

The Home page should quickly explain:

- Camron is the beekeeper behind HoneyTide.
- HoneyTide is a small growing apiary in the Springfield/Eugene area.
- Camron manages four colonies.
- The business is rooted in stewardship, learning, and responsible hive management.
- HoneyTide is growing toward future small-batch honey, beeswax, and hive products.

The Home page should not read only as an education or conservation project.

### About

The About page should remain the most personal page.

It should tell Camron’s story, why he started beekeeping, what he is learning, and why HoneyTide matters.

### Our Apiary

The Our Apiary page should feel like a documentary or field journal about the colonies.

Avoid repeating “The Teacher of...” style headings.

Preferred headings:

- Zion — The Colony That Started It All
- Tortooga — Growing at Its Own Pace
- Butera — HoneyTide’s First Swarm Capture
- The Anchor Colony — A Rescue from an Unusual Home

### Education

The Education page should focus on learning.

Topics may include:

- Honey bees
- Native bees
- Pollinator stewardship
- What a swarm is
- Pollinator-friendly habitat
- Oregon bee resources
- Future beginner classes
- Camron’s learning path through OSU and local beekeeper networks

Avoid turning the Education page into a contact page.

### Contact

The Contact page should focus on action.

It should make it easy for people to:

- Contact Camron
- Copy the HoneyTide email
- Visit the Facebook page
- Understand when Camron may be able to help with easy-access swarms
- Know what details to include when asking about a swarm
- Use OSBA swarm resources when appropriate

Do not publish Camron’s personal phone number or WhatsApp information yet.

## Repository Editing Rules

Before modifying files:

1. Read PROJECT_NOTES.md.
2. Review the current repository state.
3. Assume manually edited text is intentional.
4. Do not rewrite sections unless specifically requested.
5. Propose major copy changes before implementation.
