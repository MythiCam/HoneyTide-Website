Every Colony Has a Story

Follow the growth of HoneyTide Apiary through colony updates, swarm captures, seasonal milestones, and the lessons each hive teaches along the way.

Meet the Colonies

HoneyTide is built one colony, one inspection, and one season at a time. Each hive has its own rhythm, temperament, challenges, and lessons. Together, they tell the living story of HoneyTide’s growth as a young Oregon apiary.

Zion

Founding Colony

Zion is one of HoneyTide’s founding colonies and has become a cornerstone of the apiary’s early growth. With strong brood production, steady comb building, and healthy expansion, Zion has helped shape HoneyTide’s understanding of strength, timing, and responsible colony management.

As one of the strongest early hives, Zion continues to serve as both a productive colony and an important teacher in the apiary.

Tortooga

Founding Colony

Tortooga has been a colony of patience, observation, and careful management. While slower to develop than Zion, this hive continues to offer valuable lessons in resource balance, brood development, and the importance of listening closely to what each colony is showing.

Tortooga reminds HoneyTide that every hive grows in its own time and that good stewardship requires attention, humility, and care.

Butera

Captured Swarm

Named after the family whose property hosted the swarm, Butera represents HoneyTide’s first successful swarm capture and relocation. What began as a cluster of bees gathered on a branch became an opportunity to preserve a colony and provide it with a permanent home.

Butera is a reminder that healthy colonies continue to spread, adapt, and grow — and that beekeeping often begins by paying attention when nature presents an opportunity.

The Anchor Colony

Swarm Recovery

Originally discovered living inside a mailbox anchor, this colony became one of HoneyTide’s most memorable rescues. Their relocation reflects both the resilience of honey bees and the importance of providing safe homes for pollinators when they establish themselves in spaces shared with people.

What began as an unusual removal quickly became an opportunity to preserve a healthy colony while helping a local family and expanding HoneyTide’s experience with swarm recovery and colony relocation.

2026 Season

A Young Apiary Taking Shape

The 2026 season marks HoneyTide’s first major year of growth. From founding colonies to swarm captures and hands-on learning, this season is helping define the practices, values, and rhythm of the apiary.

Future updates will document inspections, seasonal changes, colony growth, management decisions, and the ongoing lessons that come from working closely with bees.
